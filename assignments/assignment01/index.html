Blue Light and Screen Exposure

Blue light is a type of visible light with a short wavelength that is commonly emitted by technology devices such as smartphones, computers, and televisions. Blue light plays an important role in keeping people alert during the daytime. However, research suggests that at night, exposure to blue light can raise cortisol compared to darkness, which may interfere with the body's normal wind-down process.

Why is this important?

This matters because cortisol normally drops at night so the body can relax and prepare for sleep. If cortisol stays higher, you may feel more alert, which can make it harder to fall asleep or stay asleep. Over time, poor sleep can affect mood, focus, and energy levels during the day. That is why many people try to reduce bright screen use late at night.

Blocking blue light at night

It can be helpful to reduce blue light exposure in the evening to support a consistent sleep schedule. Small changes can make a difference, especially if you use screens close to bedtime.

What can you do?

Wearing blue light blocking glasses or using screen filters can help reduce blue light exposure. Some potential benefits include:

  • Reduced eye strain during long periods of screen use.
  • Supporting healthy sleep patterns in the evening.
  • Improving visual comfort when working in bright environments.
